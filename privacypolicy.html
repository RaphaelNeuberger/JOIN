Applicable Legal Bases

Applicable legal bases under the GDPR: The following provides an overview of the legal bases of the GDPR on which personal data is processed. Please note that, in addition to the provisions of the GDPR, national data protection regulations in your or our country of residence or registered office may apply. If more specific legal bases are relevant in individual cases, they will be disclosed in this privacy policy.

  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR) – The data subject has given consent to the processing of their personal data for one or more specific purposes.
  • Performance of a contract and pre-contractual inquiries (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR) – Processing is necessary for the performance of a contract to which the data subject is party or in order to take steps at the request of the data subject prior to entering into a contract.
  • Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR) – Processing is necessary for compliance with a legal obligation to which the controller is subject.
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR) – Processing is necessary for the purposes of the legitimate interests pursued by the controller or by a third party, except where such interests are overridden by the interests or fundamental rights and freedoms of the data subject.

National data protection regulations in Germany: In addition to the GDPR, national data protection regulations apply in Germany, in particular the Federal Data Protection Act (Bundesdatenschutzgesetz – BDSG).

National data protection regulations in Liechtenstein: In addition to the GDPR, the Data Protection Act (DSG) and the Data Protection Ordinance (DSV) apply.

National data protection regulations in the Netherlands: In addition to the GDPR, the implementing act to the GDPR (Uitvoeringswet Algemene verordening gegevensbescherming – UAVG) applies.

National data protection regulations in Austria: In addition to the GDPR, the Data Protection Act (DSG) applies.

National data protection regulations in Poland: In addition to the GDPR, the Data Protection Act (Ustawa z dnia 10 maja 2018 r. o ochronie danych osobowych) applies.

Applicable legal bases under the Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP): Where applicable, processing is carried out in accordance with the Swiss FADP.

Note on applicability of the GDPR and Swiss FADP: These privacy notices serve both the GDPR and the Swiss FADP. For clarity, GDPR terminology is used.

Security Measures

Appropriate technical and organizational measures are implemented to ensure a level of protection appropriate to the risk, taking into account the state of the art, implementation costs, and the nature, scope, context and purposes of processing.

Measures include ensuring confidentiality, integrity and availability of data by controlling physical and electronic access to the data, access, input, disclosure, availability and separation of data. Procedures for exercising data subjects' rights, deletion of data and responses to data threats have been established. Data protection by design and by default is considered when selecting hardware, software and procedures.

Transfer of Personal Data

In the course of processing personal data, data may be transferred to other entities, companies, legally independent organizational units or persons, or disclosed to them. Recipients may include IT service providers or providers of services and content integrated into a website. Legal requirements are observed and appropriate agreements for the protection of your data are concluded with recipients.

International Data Transfers

Processing in third countries: Where data is processed in a third country (outside the EU/EEA) or transferred to third parties, this is done in accordance with legal requirements. Where an adequacy decision exists (Art. 45 GDPR), it serves as the basis. Otherwise, transfers occur where safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses (Art. 46(2)(c) GDPR), explicit consent, or contractual/legal necessity apply. Information is provided for each third-country provider used. See EU Commission resources for adequacy decisions.

EU-US Data Privacy Framework (DPF): Under the DPF, the EU Commission has recognized an adequate level of protection for certain US companies. Certified companies can be found at the U.S. Department of Commerce website.

Disclosure abroad under Swiss FADP: Personal data is disclosed abroad only when adequate protection is ensured (Art. 16 FADP). If no adequate protection is recognized by the Federal Council, alternative safeguards are implemented.

Rights of Data Subjects

Under the GDPR, data subjects have the rights to object, withdraw consent, access, rectification, erasure, restriction, data portability, and to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority.

Use of Cookies

Cookies store and read information on end devices for purposes such as functionality, security, convenience, and analytics. Consent is obtained where required by law; strictly necessary cookies are exempt.

Storage duration: Session cookies are deleted after closing the device; persistent cookies may remain stored (up to two years unless otherwise specified).

Withdrawal/Opt-out: Consents can be withdrawn at any time. Cookies can be restricted via browser settings. Additional opt-out options: https://optout.aboutads.info and https://www.youronlinechoices.com/.

Business Services

Data of contractual and business partners is processed to fulfill contractual obligations, safeguard rights, perform administrative tasks, and ensure security. Data is shared with third parties only as necessary and deleted according to statutory retention periods.

Provision of Online Services and Web Hosting

Users' IP addresses are processed to deliver content and functions to their browsers or devices. Server log files are kept for up to 30 days, then deleted or anonymized (unless needed for evidence).

Blogs and Publication Media

Reader data is processed only as necessary for presentation, communication, and security.

Contact and Inquiry Management

Data provided via contact channels (e.g., forms, email, phone, social media) is processed to handle requests based on legitimate interests or contract performance.

Newsletters and Electronic Notifications

Newsletters are sent with consent or legal permission, using double opt-in. Unsubscribed emails may be retained up to three years to evidence prior consent; blocklists may be used to honor objections.

Promotional Communication via Email, Mail, Fax or Phone

Personal data is processed for promotional communication. Recipients can withdraw consent or object at any time. Minimal data may be stored to avoid re-contact.

Contests and Competitions

Participant data is processed as necessary to provide, conduct, and complete contests, with deletion generally six months after the end unless longer retention is required.

Web Analytics, Monitoring and Optimization

Web analytics evaluates visitor flows and usage behavior. IP masking is used. No clear data (like names or emails) is stored; profiles are pseudonymous.

Matomo: Cookies may be set for up to 13 months. See https://matomo.org/faq/general/faq_146/.

Online Marketing

Personal data is processed for marketing purposes, including profiling and tracking, primarily using pseudonymous profiles and cookies. Opt-outs via browser settings and regional platforms are supported.

Affiliate Programs and Affiliate Links

Affiliate links may generate commissions. For attribution, third parties may learn that users followed a link within this online offering.

Customer Reviews and Rating Procedures

Participation in review platforms may involve sharing necessary data (e.g., name, email, order number) with consent to verify authenticity.

Presence on Social Networks (Social Media)

Online presences are maintained on social networks. Data may be processed outside the EU. Usage profiles may be created for market research and advertising.

Plugins and Embedded Functions and Content

Third-party content (e.g., graphics, videos, maps) is integrated, requiring processing of users' IP addresses. Providers may use pixel tags and cookies.

  • YouTube Videos: Video content from YouTube; opt-out options available.
